## Purpose

This document defines strong text quality standards for the website foundation. It is standards/framework work, not final full public copy for every route.

Do not write or approve public copy beyond existing source support. If a fact is not present, mark it `HOLD` or `MISSING_EXPECTED`.

## Writing Principles

- One URL = one primary intent.
- Start with the user's situation, not a generic service pitch.
- Explain what the page helps clarify or prepare.
- Show what information or documents may be needed without asking for public upload.
- Separate what can be said publicly from what needs review.
- Use office-first language and confirmed local facts only.
- Keep CTA language safe: `Разобрать ситуацию`, `Позвонить`, `Построить маршрут`, `Показать документы`.
- Avoid final legal, tax, bank, registry or employment conclusions.
- Use natural semantic language, not keyword stuffing.

## What Makes A Useful Page

A useful page:

- makes its route role clear in the first screen;
- tells the user when this route fits;
- explains the common situation or problem;
- states what is checked before action;
- lists possible inputs or document categories safely;
- explains the first step without promising outcome;
- routes wrong-fit users to the correct page;
- answers likely FAQ questions visibly;
- includes local contact path where appropriate;
- names limits and safety boundaries.

## Weak Or Generic Copy

Weak copy:

- says "we provide all services" without route ownership;
- repeats keywords without answering the situation;
- promises speed, results or acceptance;
- uses abstract claims like "best quality" or "trusted experts";
- hides the real next step;
- turns hubs into service catalogues;
- gives final answers where documents must be reviewed;
- adds prices, discounts, guarantees, reviews, ratings or cases;
- uses old names, old domains or state-affiliation wording.

## Keyword Use Without Stuffing

Use semantic phrases only where they fit the visible task. A page may naturally mention route terms, location and document context, but should not repeat a query pattern mechanically.

Rules:

- title, H1 and first screen must support one intent;
- hubs use route-selection language;
- money pages use exact-intent language;
- diagnostics use "what affects the answer" language;
- contacts use NAP/contact language;
- `/policy` uses legal/privacy language only;
- live SERP facts and search volume remain absent until checked.

## Yandex-Oriented Usefulness

For Yandex SEO, visible content should help the user choose the right route without over-solving:

- show page role quickly;
- include clear section headings;
- add visible FAQ before any FAQ schema;
- keep breadcrumbs and related routes human-readable;
- use confirmed local cues only;
- avoid hidden SEO text;
- avoid long generic intros.

## Safe CTA Rules

Allowed CTA hierarchy:

- main: `Разобрать ситуацию`;
- secondary: `Позвонить`, `Построить маршрут`, `Показать документы`.

CTA copy must not imply:

- guaranteed result;
- free full service;
- legal/tax/bank outcome;
- exact deadline;
- file upload;
- CRM/live submission success before backend acceptance.

## FAQ Rules

FAQ should:

- answer real uncertainty from the route source;
- stay visible if schema is used;
- be short, specific and safe;
- route users to the next safe step;
- say "depends on inputs/documents" when needed.

FAQ must not:

- promise outcomes;
- publish prices or deadlines;
- give legal/tax conclusions;
- contain hidden schema-only answers;
- capture another route's intent.

## Process And Document Block Rules

Process blocks should describe sequence, not outcome:

1. Understand the situation.
2. Check source document or inputs.
3. Identify the route.
4. Prepare or clarify needed materials.
5. Agree next step.

Document/input blocks may list categories, not personal or client documents. They must say no public upload unless a separately approved secure flow exists.

## Uncertainty And HOLD Handling

Use uncertainty honestly:

- "зависит от вводных";
- "нужно посмотреть документ";
- "маршрут выбирается после разбора";
- "точный вывод не делается на публичной странице".

If a fact is missing, use `HOLD` in source docs or omit from public copy.

## Prohibited Claims

Do not write:

- prices or price ranges;
- discounts or promotions;
- guarantees;
- exact deadlines;
- urgent-result promises;
- reviews, ratings or cases;
- official state affiliation;
- legal entity details, INN, OGRN or requisites;
- working hours, office number or floor;
- final legal/tax/bank/registry outcomes;
- claims wider than visible confirmed content.

## Page Block Models

### Homepage

Purpose: brand/local router, not a services catalogue.

Required blocks:

1. Brand/local entry.
2. Route selector.
3. Situation review CTA.
4. Priority route cards.
5. Process overview.
6. Documents/materials preview.
7. Local contact.
8. Safety message.

Rules:

- first screen must signal `Документы для бизнеса`, Simferopol and safe first step;
- route cards are limited and purposeful;
- do not list every service as a catalogue;
- local marker may use `офис рядом с налоговой` without state-affiliation wording.

### Hub Page

Purpose: choose route for mixed intent.

Required blocks:

1. Route-selector hero.
2. User situations.
3. Route grid.
4. How to choose.
5. Child route previews.
6. Anti-cannibalization boundaries.
7. Fallback to situation review/contact.

Rules:

- hub does not duplicate child money-page H1 or title;
- child cards explain differences;
- related links are limited and useful.

### Money Page

Purpose: exact commercial intent.

Required blocks:

1. Exact-intent hero.
2. When this page fits.
3. User situation/problem.
4. What we check.
5. What documents/data may be needed.
6. How work starts.
7. What is not promised.
8. Related routes.
9. Local contact.
10. FAQ.
11. Safety note.

Rules:

- keep exact intent narrow;
- include "what is not promised";
- related routes must clarify alternatives;
- no outcome, deadline, price or guarantee claims.

### Diagnostic Page

Purpose: applicability, influence or calculation intent without final public conclusion.

Required blocks:

1. Diagnostic question.
2. When it matters.
3. What affects the answer.
4. What information is needed.
5. Next safe route.
6. Limits of public page.
7. FAQ.

Rules:

- do not publish final advice or fake calculator output;
- route to hub, money page or `/razbor-situacii/` after inputs.

### Contact Page

Purpose: canonical NAP and safe contact path.

Required blocks:

1. NAP.
2. Phone CTA.
3. Route/map guidance only if confirmed.
4. Safe contact note.
5. Policy link.
6. No unconfirmed hours/floor/office.

Rules:

- no working hours, floor, office number, legal details or requisites;
- route/map provider dependency stays gated until approved.

### Policy Page

Purpose: legal/privacy transparency.

Required blocks:

- policy and data handling content from legal source;
- safe contact fallback only;
- no aggressive conversion;
- no service SEO targeting;
- no broader claims than owner/legal-approved text.

## Acceptance Criteria

Text foundation is acceptable when:

- every page model has visible route purpose;
- copy rules preserve anti-cannibalization;
- CTA hierarchy is safe;
- FAQ and document blocks avoid overclaims;
- HOLD boundaries are explicit;
- no final route copy is invented beyond source support;
- public launch remains blocked.
