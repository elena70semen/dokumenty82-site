## 5. Route Quality Scoring Model

Each indexed route is reviewed across these dimensions:

| Dimension | What good looks like | Unsafe failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Route intent clarity | One visible main task and no neighbouring intent capture. | Hub becomes money page, or money page becomes broad catalogue. |
| Specificity of "when page fits" | 2-4 client-language situations tied to this URL. | Generic "we help with everything" copy. |
| Specificity of "what we check" | Route-specific inputs, source document, period, status or request wording. | Final tax/legal/bank/registry conclusion. |
| Documents/data usefulness | Safe categories that help the user prepare. | Public upload, credentials, personal data or exhaustive universal list. |
| How work starts | Calm first step: describe, show safely, choose route, agree next step. | Fake success, live form, CRM submission or pressure wording. |
| What is not promised | Clear boundary around external outcome, timing, price and final conclusion. | Guarantees, urgent-result promise or external acceptance claim. |
| Related-route quality | Parent/child/sibling routes explain the difference. | Flat route wall or noindex route exposed. |
| FAQ usefulness | Questions answer route fit, inputs, differences and safe next step. | Price/deadline/outcome answers or hidden schema-only FAQ. |
| CTA safety | Approved labels and destinations only. | Unapproved CTA, messaging link, upload or live endpoint. |
| HOLD safety | No HOLD facts exposed. | Legal IDs, hours, office/floor, prices, reviews, state wording. |
| Mobile readability | Short lists, stable cards and no overflow. | Long walls, repeated keyword blocks or controls that wrap badly. |

No route is marked fully approved. Passing this scoring model can only produce `READY_FOR_OWNER_REVIEW`.

Allowed statuses:

- `READY_FOR_OWNER_REVIEW`
- `NEEDS_CONTENT_DEEPENING`
- `NEEDS_SOURCE_CLARIFICATION`
- `BLOCKED`
- `NOT_PUBLIC_LIVE_READY`

## 7. What Makes A Route Page Useful

A useful route page:

- states the route's owned situation early;
- names when the route fits;
- says what is checked before document preparation;
- tells the user what information may help;
- explains how work starts without a live form promise;
- names what is not promised;
- links to parent, child or sibling routes with a reason;
- gives FAQ directions that answer real uncertainty;
- ends with approved CTAs only;
- keeps sensitive materials office-first or agreed-channel only.

## 8. What Makes A Route Page Unsafe

Unsafe content includes:

- outcome promise for IFNS, bank, registry, tax, HR or another external side;
- price, discount, guarantee, exact deadline or urgent-result claim;
- unapproved legal identifier, working hours, office/floor, representative name or requisites;
- public upload or live endpoint;
- fake form success;
- analytics or CRM activation;
- messaging deep link;
- hidden SEO-only block;
- noindex foundation route in public navigation or sitemap;
- HTTP/3/QUIC/UDP/443 activation or advertisement without owner/ops approval.

## 10. FAQ Quality Requirements

FAQ topics should cover:

- when this route is right;
- what information to prepare;
- how this route differs from parent or sibling routes;
- what remains dependent on documents;
- how to start safely.

FAQ must not answer:

- exact price;
- exact deadline;
- guaranteed result;
- final legal/tax/bank/registry conclusion;
- unsupported provider, channel or public-profile claim.
